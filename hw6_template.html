This map shows how many housesholds received SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) benefits in each county in North Carolina in 2020. The SNAP program helps eligible low-income households and individuals purchase food. In North Carolina, eligibilty for SNAP benefits is based on an individual having a combined checking and savingss account total less than $2,001 or a maximum annual income of $27,180. These monetary limits increase as the number of individuals in a household increases.
